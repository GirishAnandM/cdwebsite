About Carpediem

The Story

Stylised "Carpediem", the biennial cult fest of CBIT, conducted by CS is truly a marvel of the combination of sheer effort and the thinking power and innovation of the brightest of today's youth. The idea of holding a large-scale events that mirrored and even boosted CBIT's reputation as one of the powerhouses of Indian education was conceived during 2002 and taking inspiration from the ancient poet Horace's quotation, Carpediem 2002 was a roaring success on an unprecedented scale.

CBIT

Nestled between swathes of open land and the serene Osman Sagar Lake, Chaitanya Bharathi Institute of Technology (CBIT) lies on the outer reaches of Hyderabad, close to the famous financial district which is home to the offices of major IT companies.

Set up in 1979, CBIT immediately became one of the most noteworthy private engineering colleges in the city and rose to become the state's foremost institute of technology, aiming to compete with the likes of IIT's and BITS. Known primarily for being a consistent producer of academically brilliant students, CBIT is also a melting pot for a diverse student community, the likes of which is hard to find anywhere else in Andhra Pradesh. Comprising of students from disparate backgrounds as well as a healthy dose of foreign students, the cultural diversity of CBIT is an asset to the college.

Chaitanya Smruti

Chaitanya Smruti (CS) was established by the outgoing students of the first intake, the class of '79 immediately upon their graduation in 1983. Chaitanya Smruti grew into more than just the alumni body and now represents the students and their interest in co-curricular activities.

Student Body

CS plays a heavy role in many of the college's social, environmental and cultural initiatives, with the students going out on plantation drives, blood donations and a multitude of cultural events which aim to raise money for a social cause.

The student body of Chaitanya Smruti is the chief functionary organ in assembling the funds, artistes and manpower to hold the college's most famous cultural contribution, Carpediem.
